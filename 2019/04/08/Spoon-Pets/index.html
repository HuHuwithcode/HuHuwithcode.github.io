Spoon Pets

Posted on 2019-04-08 | Edited on 2019-04-14 | In Completed Projects | Comments: | Views:

[July 2018 - November 2018]
Course: Human-Computer Interaction

“Spoon Pets” is a conceptual interaction design of an application prototype that helps users develop productivity and overcome procrastination, therefore enables them to foster ethical behaviour. The prototype was built by using Adobe suit and sketch.

Introduction

Spoon Pets is a conceptual interaction design of an application that helps users develop productivity and overcome procrastination, therefore enables them to foster ethical behaviour. The system is targeted at procrastinators, including people with poor time-management, low self-discipline and poor memory. The system uses a working method called Pomodoro Technique to assist users to complete their tasks, using a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks, while setting limitation in application to control users’ mobile phone and also to remind users what they should do every day. Also, users will be rewarded by virtual coins that allow users to keep virtual pets, which can be later turned into real money to help animals in the streets under the support of Stray Animal Care Organizations. This can act as a motivation to keep users participating.

Project Description

Milestone 1: Conceptual Design & Analysis

To comprehend the system and the demand of users, we did a survey online and the face-to-face interview
with different types of people. After we researched and analysed those data, we founnd out the main function
that user needed and the problem that the existing system and application lacked. In the last, we
complete a simple prototype and give the detail and description of our conceptual model. Our process included five steps and we integrated them into the next four sections which are the contextual inquiry, contextual analysis, conceptual model and low fidelity prototype.

Milestone 2: Conceptual Design & Prototype

Our team provided a conceptual design for the system that is being developed including a rationale for that design based in the information obtained from the contextual inquiry. It was also incorporate considerations arising from the interaction paradigm, interaction mode, context of use and the results from completing an evaluation of a low fidelity prototype by targeted users, and analysis of the results from that evaluation. In this step, the design process concluded with revised key user needs and goals, and system requirements including our team’s rationale.

Milestone 3: Evaluation

Our team provided the results of completing evaluations of the interactive system. The evaluations include: usability evaluations such as heuristic evaluations, cognitive walkthroughs and pluralistic evaluations, as well as UX evaluations. The research process incorporate an analysis of the data we had collected (both quantitative and qualitative), outline our primary findings, and provide recommendations. Additionally, the analysis included measures such as success rates, task time and error rates as well as assessment of UX goals.
