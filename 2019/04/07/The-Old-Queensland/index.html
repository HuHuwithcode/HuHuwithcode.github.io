The Old Queensland

Posted on 2019-04-07 | Edited on 2019-04-14 | In Completed Projects | Comments: | Views:

[July 2017 - November 2017]
Course: Design Computing Studio 1

The Old Queensland is an interactive web application built using the State Library of Queensland. SLQ data (http://data.gov.au/organization/slq). The primary audience of this website should be teenagers, especially for the young generation, Our team wish they can use new technology to engage with history. It utilises SLQ data via data.gov.au’s APIs for dynamic searching. The data is divided into three sections on the website, including Image Gallery, News Photo, and Soldier Portrait, users can search and filter preferred information by choosing different years and topic options. The objective of the website is allowing users to explore historical information through various interesting interactions, visualizing digital records and offering an aesthetic visual representation for users. At the meantime, this project tends to provide a better browsing and learning experience for the target customers of the website.

Introduction

Our team has established an interactive website which is basically a showcase of Queensland’s information during the First World War. The primary audience of this website should be teenagers, the data which applied are all from government databases in Queensland. The data will be divided into three sections on the website, including Image Gallery, News Photo, and Soldier Portrait, users can search and filter preferred information by choosing different years and other options. The objective of the website is allowing users to explore historical information through various interesting interactions, visualizing digital records and offering an aesthetic visual representation for users. At the meantime, this project tends to provide a better browsing and learning experience for the target customers of the website.

Project Description

Interesting Aspect

In terms of general impression, history has always been solemn and inviolable. Using the project of our group, the user can be able to cross the time and geographical restriction, browse and access interactive forms of specific historical information. We hope this interactive website can help target audience quering and browsing their interested information more conveniently and effectively. Through the images, photos and other visual design elements to attract potential users to browse our website, search and find information about the history of Queensland in World War I. In the process of browsing, the users will remember and memorize the specific time of important events and warriors.

This historical digital interactive website shows the “interactive” animation and historical information which includes integrated science, knowledge and interests. Through digital exhibition interactive, cutting-edge digital content viewing mode and other advanced exhibition methods, the website enables users to experience an impressive journey of technological history. The website becomes more comprehensive and attractive because of the high integration of various elements. Through the combination of video, sound, animation and variety of media methods, it intensively shows the background and significance of Queensland in World War I. The project is expected to bring back historical events, historical figures, historical objects and other historical elements by using the latest design concept and display technology. Through the interaction with this website, history is no longer far away from us or out of people’s imagination, it is being exhibited right in front of the users, granting them a deeper understanding of these classic and unforgettable memories.

Conclusion

Ultimately, we anticipate this project will promote and encourage users to improve their autonomous learning and digital reading ability by fully using digital reading interactivity. By using the website of digitized historical information, we can cultivate the target groups include teenagers and children to control and apply the advanced learning strategies such as summarization and self-regulation. Finally, these users can make conscious control of their reading motivation and reading strategies, which will help them reading more actively and efficiently through browsing the historical information of digital media.
